12 Astonishing Facts About Erawan Falls

Publish date: 0001-01-01
Source: Tripadvisor.com

Erawan Falls is one of the most mesmerizing and awe-inspiring natural landmarks in Thailand. Located in the western part of the country, in the Erawan National Park, this majestic waterfall is a true gem of the Kanchanaburi province. It attracts thousands of visitors every year who come to witness its enchanting beauty and experience the tranquility of the surrounding nature.

But Erawan Falls is more than just a picturesque waterfall. This natural wonder is steeped in fascinating history and boasts some incredible facts that will leave you astonished. From its striking seven-tiered structure to its crystal-clear turquoise waters, Erawan Falls has captivated adventurers and nature lovers for decades.

In this article, we will delve into 12 astonishing facts about Erawan Falls that will showcase the unique allure and significance of this iconic landmark. So, let’s dive into this immersive experience and explore the wonders of Erawan Falls!

Erawan Falls gets its name from Hindu mythology.

The falls are named after Erawan, a three-headed elephant in Hindu mythology, which is believed to represent the Hindu god Indra. The multiple tiers of the waterfall are said to resemble the three heads of the elephant.

It is one of the most famous waterfalls in Thailand.

Erawan Falls is renowned for its stunning beauty and is considered one of the most famous waterfalls in Thailand. It attracts thousands of tourists each year who come to witness its enchanting charm.

The waterfall is part of the Erawan National Park.

Erawan Falls is located within the Erawan National Park, which spans over 550 square kilometers. The park is home to diverse flora and fauna, including monkeys, birds, and butterflies.

Erawan Falls consists of seven tiers.

The waterfall is made up of seven distinct tiers, each with its own unique beauty. Visitors can hike up to the higher levels to discover hidden pools and smaller cascades.

The water is known for its stunning turquoise color.

One of the most mesmerizing features of Erawan Falls is the color of its water. The turquoise hue is a result of the mineral deposits from the limestone rocks.

Visitors can swim in the lower tiers.

Unlike some waterfalls where swimming is prohibited, Erawan Falls allows visitors to take a refreshing dip in the lower tiers. The cool, clear water provides a welcome respite from the tropical heat.

Erawan Falls is home to various species of fish.

The pools and streams in Erawan Falls are home to several species of fish, including the famous doctor fish. These tiny fish are known for their unique ability to nibble on dead skin, offering a natural fish spa experience.

There are hiking trails leading to the upper tiers of the waterfall.

For those seeking adventure, Erawan Falls offers hiking trails that lead to the higher tiers of the waterfall. These trails offer opportunities for breathtaking views and encounters with wildlife.

The falls are surrounded by lush greenery.

Erawan Falls is nestled within a lush forest, providing a picturesque backdrop to the cascading waterfalls. The surrounding greenery adds to the tranquility and natural beauty of the area.

The first tier of Erawan Falls is the most popular.

While all seven tiers of Erawan Falls are stunning, the first tier is the most accessible and therefore the most popular among visitors. It features a large pool where visitors can swim and relax.

The waterfall is considered sacred by the locals.

The local Thai people hold Erawan Falls in high regard and consider it a sacred place. It is believed to have spiritual significance and is often visited by locals for prayers and offerings.

The journey to Erawan Falls is as enchanting as the destination itself.

As you make your way to Erawan Falls, you will be surrounded by lush forests, vibrant wildlife, and stunning landscapes. The journey itself is an experience that adds to the allure of visiting this natural wonder.

FAQs

1. How do I get to Erawan Falls?

To reach Erawan Falls, you can take a bus or taxi from Kanchanaburi town, located about 63 kilometers away. The journey takes approximately one and a half to two hours, depending on the traffic. Entrance to the falls is at the Erawan National Park, where you will need to purchase an admission ticket.

2. Are there any facilities available at Erawan Falls?

Yes, there are several facilities available at Erawan Falls, including toilets, changing rooms, and picnic areas. There are also food stalls and souvenir shops near the entrance of the park, where you can grab a bite to eat or purchase souvenirs.

3. Is swimming allowed at Erawan Falls?

Yes, swimming is permitted at Erawan Falls. The lower tiers of the waterfall have designated swimming areas where you can take a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear waters. However, it is important to be cautious and follow safety guidelines provided by park officials.

4. Are there any hiking trails at Erawan Falls?

Yes, there are several hiking trails at Erawan Falls that allow visitors to explore the surrounding jungle and admire the natural beauty of the area. The trails range in difficulty, catering to both beginners and experienced hikers. Make sure to wear appropriate footwear and carry drinking water during your hike.

5. Can I camp at Erawan Falls?

No, camping is not allowed at Erawan Falls. However, if you wish to spend the night near the falls, there are accommodation options available in the nearby town of Kanchanaburi. You can choose from a range of hotels, guesthouses, and resorts to suit your preferences and budget.
