The 20 Best Pajamas for Women of 2024

Publish date: 2024-08-27

Let me start off by saying that I live for sleep. After experiencing my first burnout from lack of sleep at the mere age of 24, I vowed to prioritize good rest. No Netflix show, no gripping book, and no night out with friends will rob me of my sleep. Just me in a set of cozy pajamas to help me drift away into REM sleep.

And I’m not the only one with this sentiment. In her book “Thrive,” Arianna Huffington writes about the importance of having a trusty pajama set to turn to when night falls, because, at minimum, it serves as a signal to her body that it's time to "shut down" and assist her in getting a good night's rest. To her point, great pajamas should make one comfortable enough to doze off ASAP and sleep uninterrupted by itchiness, chills, or excessive body heat.

Basically, bedtime-wear should be something we look forward to putting on each evening — good sleep depends on it. For some that may mean opting for bamboo pajamas or silk sets, but if you don't have any specific material requirement, any of the following cloud-like pajamas will feel great on the skin. And while lines have been getting a little blurry between loungewear and sleepwear lately, we're firm believers that there needs to be a clear distinction between bed clothes and couch clothes. To help you find your perfect pajamas, we sourced options that are comfortable, warm, and stylish.

What to Keep in Mind

Material

If you get a recommended eight hours of sleep every night, you sleep for four months of the year. So, you should probably spend that time on quality materials that encourage rest, right? As a general rule of thumb, we suggest you opt for natural fibers like cotton, silk, linen, and bamboo. But your sleeping habits impact which material you’ll love the most.

Anyone who experiences night sweats needs bamboo material, a moisture-absorbing fabric that you can find in the Cozy Earth Short Sleeve Bamboo Pajama Set because it will take in the excess water and keep your temperature regulated. Those who tend to overheat, however, or like to go to bed in a cool environment, should opt for a lightweight fabric like linen instead. The Bed Threads French Flax Linen Long Sleeve Shirt and The Lazy Poet Emma Olive Zebra Linen Pajamas keep airflow moving in their breathable fabric. For something a little warmer and softer, cotton options like the Petite Plume Luxe Pima Cotton Wide Leg Pajamas can keep you cozy. Some just like to have a luxurious sleeping experience (I mean, who doesn’t). Silk will feel buttery soft on the skin and can transition you from wake to sleep in an instant. Allow the Lunya Washable Silk Set or the Intimissimi Velvet Paisley Satin Jacquard Top to lull you to sleep.

Fit

Since comfort can not be compromised when it comes to pajamas, it is crucial to consider the right fit. Pajamas that have a looser cut, like the Commesi La Boxer Alta, Eberjey Gisele Long Pj Set, and CottonOn Flannel Boyfriend Boxer Pants, will likely sit more comfortably around you. For those that are mildly more form-fitting, like the Anthropologie Silky Lace Slip Dress, it might make sense to size up for a more relaxed fit. Remember that those that come with drawstrings will always make it easier to adjust for size, allowing you to take a little more risk when making your purchasing decisions. 

Your Questions, Answered

What are the best pajamas for night sweats?

Certain materials can feel more comfortable for night sweats. Stylist Michaela Leitz has a preference for fabrics like “linen, cotton, and silk,” adding, “Especially silk will feel cooler against your skin, which is perfect for hot summer nights, and linen and cotton will absorb sweat very easily. When we sweat during the night, natural fibers are ideal as they are more breathable, sweat absorbing, and must be cleaned less often than synthetics.” Wellness influencer Arina Negisihi seconds this statement, explaining, “I get hot and cold while sleeping, so cotton is the best when it comes to trapping and wicking moisture from my body.” Options to try include The Lazy Poet Emma Olive Zebra Linen Pajamas and the Lunya Washable Silk Set.

What are the best pajamas for winter?

Leitz recommends natural fibers for winter pajamas, explaining, “These fabrics are not only warm but cozy against your skin.” Cotton can be woven into a thicker material, which makes it a great year-round fabric to turn to. Nia the Brand Joan Sweater is a great winter pajama that is thick and soft. Theodora exclusively sleeps in cotton and silk. “Cotton is really great for temperature regulating — I also make sure my bedding is 100 percent cotton,” she explains. 

What pajamas should I wear for body acne?

If your concerns during the winter surround your skin more than sleep quality, Theodora recommends silk as it “keeps the skin soft.” The Lunya Washable Silk Set, which happens to be one of her favorite pajama brands, is ideal for maintaining smooth skin texture. What causes skin breakouts on the body are oils, sweat, dirt, and dust, which materials such as cotton absorb and become a breeding ground for bacteria. Silk does not absorb oil, dirt, mold, or allergens and can keep the skin cool and dry throughout the night.

Dr. Elaine F. Kung says she loves silk and another material, linen, for this reason. Sleeping in linen leads to less sweating, which results in fewer body acne breakouts. "It’s one of the most eco-friendly and cleanest fabrics due to its antibacterial and dirt-repellent properties,” Kung says. Try it via Bed Threads' French Flax Linen Long Sleeve Shirt or The Lazy Poet's Emma Olive Zebra Linen Pajamas.
