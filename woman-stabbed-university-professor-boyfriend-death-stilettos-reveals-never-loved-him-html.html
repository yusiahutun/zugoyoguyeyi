Woman who 'stabbed university professor boyfriend to death with stiletto' reveals she never loved him

A woman accused of stabbing her boyfriend in the face and neck 25 times with her stiletto, killing him, has revealed that she never loved him.

In a Houston court on Wednesday, jurors heard Ana Trujillo, 45, describe the moment she stabbed Alf Stefan Andersson to death last June in a police interview shortly after the killing.

She also revealed that she had never been romantically interested in Andersson, a University of Houston professor, but agreed to move in with him to see if she could fall in love with him.

'I wanted to work with him,' Trjullo, who worked as an artist, said in the recorded statement. 'I didn't want to be romantically involved with him. I was not interested in being with an older gentleman.'

'Murder weapon': Prosecutors say Ana Lilia Trujillo used this stiletto to repeatedly stab her boyfriend following an argument last June

But she said that Andersson, 59, fell in love with her and she agreed to live with him to see if she felt the same way, the Houston Chronicle reported.

She said that Andersson had told her he wanted to marry her but added that he was emotionally abusive, would yell at her and struggled with alcoholism.

He also showered her with expensive gifts; a month before his death, he bought her a pair of $1,500 stilettos and a $4,000 bag, but she said she only later learned of their steep costs.

Before his murder last year, the couple had been out drinking when a man offered to buy her a drink.

Back at their apartment, Andersson attacked her - grabbing her, wrestling her to the ground and suffocating her - because he thought she was going to leave him, she said.

She told investigators that she grabbed the 5-inch high-heeled shoe and began swinging it behind her in an effort to get him off her.

Convicted: Ana Trujillo sits in court before opening arguments in her trial in Houston. On Tuesday she was convicted of killing her boyfriend with a stiletto

Killed: Trujillo claimed that she stabbed Alf Stefan Andersson, pictured, in self defense

Emotional: Trujillo, left, reacts to hearing the 911 call recording during her trial in Houston on Tuesday

'I was begging him to get off of me because I couldn't breathe,' she said. 'I hit him a couple of times.'

Andersson lost his balance and she ended up sitting on his chest and yelled that she would only get off him if he stopped hurting her, she said.

On Tuesday, prosecutors showed jurors the blood-stained navy pump, which was found beside Andersson's body by police officers after the death on June 9.

Jurors were also shown a crime scene video of Andersson's apartment that captured his bludgeoned body in a pool of blood.

The 911 call Trujillo made after the early-morning attack was also played in court. As she listened, she wept and held a tissue to her face.

It was difficult to understand Trujillo on the call, and the 911 operator seemed to be unsure if she was reporting an assault or that someone was injured and needed medical help.

When the operator realized someone needed medical attention, she transferred the call to medical services, which directed Trujillo to perform CPR. Trujillo said she was already doing that.

'I need help,' she could be heard saying on the recording between cries and deep breaths. 'I hit him with my shoe. He's on the floor, he's bleeding a lot.'

Trujillo's attorney Jack Carroll is expected to make the same tape part of her claim of self-defense.

On Monday, her lawyers said she stabbed Andersson, 59, because she couldn't handle his physical attacks anymore.

Prosecutor Sarah Mickelson on Monday said the two had a fractious relationship and that Trujillo had a history of losing her temper with Andersson.

The couple got into an argument after an evening of heavy drinking.

Trujillo and Andersson had spent most of the evening at Bodegas, a local taco shop, where they drank a couple bottles of wine and took a few tequila shots.

But things turned sour that night when another man approached Trujillo at the bar and offered to buy her a drink. Andersson got jealous of the stranger's advances which spurned a fight back at his apartment around 2 a.m.

Fallout: Before the murder, the couple had been drinking heavily at this taco restaurant when a man offered to buy Trujillo a drink, which sparked a fight back at the couple's home later that night

The ensuing fight was so loud that neighbors thought it sounded like furniture moving.

When Andersson grabbed her, Trujillo told police that she pulled his hair - so much so that she had clumps of it in her hands. When that didn't stop him she started beating him with the shoe.

Scene: She called police to the couple's apartment where they found his bludgeoned body

Trujillo phoned police just before 4am to the victim's high rise building and answered the door covered in blood.

'It looked like something out of a horror movie,' Assistant Harris County District Attorney Sarah Mickelson said. 'There was so much blood, the police officer thought Stefan had been shot in the face.'

Mickelson said Andersson had been stabbed and was 'beaten to bloody pulp.'

Upon entering, police found Swedish Anderson in a hallway, with Trujillo's stiletto next to him.

The police report detailed about 10 puncture wounds to the head as well as 15 to 20 wounds on his face and neck.

Trujillo, a mother of two who's out on bail, has pleaded not guilty. She was acting in self-defense, her attorney, Jack Carroll, said.

Trujillo admitted in June that she killed Andersson in a lover's spat at their apartment.

While Trujillo claims she acted in self-defense, that doesn't change the charges, according to Sara Marie Kinney, a spokesman for the Harris County District Attorney's office.

'She confessed to doing it. The reason and all of that will play out in court,' Kinney said.
