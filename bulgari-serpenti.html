The Bulgari Serpenti where to buy and what to know

Publish date: 2024-06-10

Bulgari, an Italian icon of fine jewellery making, has reached an important milestone. This year celebrates the 75th anniversary of its Serpenti collection. One of the maison’s most cherished motifs, the Serpenti epitomises all things Bulgari – passion for colour, flair for combining exotic materials, innovative artistry and effortless glamour – and the collection has become one of the most well-known and beloved symbols in modern jewellery.

Since the first iterations appeared in the late 1940s, the Serpenti has been worn by some of Hollywood’s biggest names and boasts a historic legacy to match.

Here, we delve into the fascinating history of the design, and what makes the collection such an enduring classic.

Jeremy Moeller//Getty Images

The symbolism of the snake

Serpents have been celebrated across myriad cultures for centuries, with precious snake relics dating back to Ancient Egypt. Wisdom, eternity, strength, rebirth and protection are just a few of the powerful qualities attributed to snake talismans throughout history.

The Greek goddess of love and beauty, Aphrodite, was often pictured wearing a snake bracelet and serpents were an important emblem during the Roman Empire, too. Snakes have also been cherished by many monarchs, giving them a sense of powerful prestige and regality – Queen Victoria notably received a snake silhouette engagement ring from Prince Albert in 1839, bestowed as a symbol of everlasting love.

The growing popularity of the Bulgari Serpenti

The first collection of Serpenti designs arrived in 1948, comprising an array of bracelet-style watches that put the now famous ‘Tubogas’ technique on the map. True to the ‘Retro’ or ‘Machine Style’ of the era, the ‘Tubogas’ bracelet was an avant-garde technique inspired by coiled gas pipe. This technique gave each design a sprung and sinuous silhouette like the world had never seen – soon, Serpenti designs were in high demand.

The influential Diana Vreeland – fashion editor of Harper's Bazaar US from 1936 until 1962, and later editor-in-chief of US Vogue –was a key champion of the Serpenti. She didn’t mention the collection by name, but famously said to her team of editors in 1968: "The serpent should be on every finger and all wrists and all everywhere." Vreeland’s personal jewellery collection reflected this mood – she was renowned for wearing a striking Serpenti belt audaciously looped twice around her neck.

It was indeed the 1960s that cemented the Serpenti as an icon – Italian glamour had gripped American culture and key cinematic moments anchored the Serpenti in the very history of the silver screen. Most notably, Elizabeth Taylor wore a Serpenti design on the set of Cleopatra in 1962, and her lover Richard Burton (who famously showered Taylor with jewelled gifts) quipped: "The only Italian Elizabeth knows is Bulgari."

By the 1970s, bold colour had become synonymous with the maison and maximalist Serpenti designs followed suit – and then came the 1980s' iterations that bore the ‘Bulgari Bulgari’ logo that is now instantly recognisable.

Today, the Serpenti motif inspires both one-of-a-kind jewelled masterpieces and everyday collections, with a broad range of timepieces, rings, pendants, earrings and bracelets that riff on the Serpenti theme.

Who wears Serpenti?

The list of Serpenti devotees reads like a who’s who of fashion, the arts and pop culture, both then and now. In the 1980s, Andy Warhol famously wore a triple-coil Tubogas style snake watch and stopped by Bulgari each time he visited Rome, as he believed it to be "the most important museum of contemporary art". The Serpenti has continued to garner cinematic appearances too; in 2006 Meryl Streep wore a steel Serpenti watch in The Devil Wears Prada, bringing the design to a new generation of fashion enthusiasts.

Today, the Serpenti still makes headlines with awards season appearances – in 2021 Zendaya walked the red carpet of the Venice International Film Festival wearing the jaw-dropping ‘Magnifica Hypnotic’ – a diamond necklace featuring an astonishing emerald weighing almost 94 carats.

Coupled with Dua Lipa, Miley Cyrus and Bella Hadid, the Serpenti remains steadfastly upon fashion’s most influential elite for the red carpet, but thanks to the brand’s Serpenti line of handbags, the motif also accompanies the likes of Kate Moss, Miranda Kerr and Priyanka Chopra during dressed-down moments, too.

Where to buy the Serpenti?

You can invest in Serpenti watches and jewels directly from Bulgari, or through major designer retailers, including Selfridges, Net-a-Porter and Harrods. You can also pick up pre-loved options from the likes of Vestiaire Collective, Farfetch, Watchfinder and 1st Dibs. And don't forget, whenever you invest in a forever piece, whether it’s watches or jewellery, it’s important to know how to correctly store and look after it to ensure it stands the test of time.
