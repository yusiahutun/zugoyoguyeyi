Housewife 'remarries' wealthy business mogul while raking in hundreds of thousands of dollars from real husband

A housewife has been taken to court after marrying two wealthy men and taking money from both, revelling in a lavish lifestyle of pricey jewellery and five-star trips.

Luxury: Mrs Silber lives in a million-dollar home and drives a Cadillac Escalade, which cost about $65,000

Eda Silber, from Long Island, New York, reportedly enjoyed more than $300,000 in support payments from her first husband.

At the same time, she was living in luxury courtesy of her second, who showered her with designer clothes, fur coats and trips across the globe.

A court ruled that she can no longer receive the $60,000-a-year payments but can keep what she has already taken, the New York Post reported.

And the court, in Nassau, threw out bigamy charges after her nuptials to her second 'husband' last April were deemed not legally binding.

Silber, 37, married her first husband, Daniel Silber, in 1995 and they had four children together, according to the Post.

She left him in 2006 and moved into the home of her wealthy boyfriend, pillow mogul George Frankel, 51, in Lawrence, New York.

Daniel Silber sued for divorce but his wife avoided the case with legal maneouvering, said hearing officer Stanley Gartenstein, the Post reported.

At the same time, she enjoyed Tiffany jewellery, a $12,000 fur coat and Jimmy Choo shoes from Frenkel, according to court papers.

The duo also visited five-star hotels in Hong Kong, Las Vegas, Puerto Rico and Israel.

They live in a million-dollar home, while Silber drives a Cadillac Escalade, which cost upwards of $65,000.

Taking the pricey gifts was 'double-dipping’ and 'must be deemed in bad faith', Gartenstein ruled.

Silber, who is an Orthodox Jew, 'married’ her boyfriend in a religious service last April, according to court papers.

'Her strategy backfired. She had all of her necessities and luxuries paid for by her parents and her boyfriend, but she wasn’t satisfied'

Stephen Gassman, husband's lawyer

But the court ruled that, based on Judaism's prohibition against marriage with someone who has already shared 'intimacy' while married to another person, the vows did not constitute a marriage, the Post reported.

The judge said the 'marriage' was not valid under civil or religious law.

Daniel Silber’s lawyer, Stephen Gassman, said: 'I think her strategy backfired. She had all of her necessities and luxuries paid for by her parents and her boyfriend, but she wasn’t satisfied.'

Eda’s lawyer, Robert Hiltzik, branded the decision 'outrageous'.

The divorce from her first husband will be finalised in a week, and he must continue to keep up support payments for their children.
