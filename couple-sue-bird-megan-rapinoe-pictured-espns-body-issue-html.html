Couple Sue Bird and Megan Rapinoe pictured in ESPN's Body Issue

Publish date: 2024-02-02

Athletic superstars Sue Bird and Megan Rapinoe have become the first ever gay couple to appear on the cover of ESPN The Magazine's annual Body Issue. 

Basketball player Sue, 37, and soccer player Megan, 32 - who have five Olympic gold medals between the two of them - officially announced they were a couple in July 2017 after several months of dating.

In an interview with ESPN's Jemele Hill, the couple admitted they were surprised the magazine wanted both of them for the cover. 'To have this really unique vehicle to actually do that, to celebrate who we are in our sport, but also the fact that we'll be the first gay couple, is pretty special,' Megan said.

Athletes: Sue (left) plays basketball for the Seattle Storm while Megan (right) plays soccer for the Seattle Reign FC 

Intimate: The couple said they were honored to be featured for ESPN The Magazine's Body Issue

'Not only are we female athletes, but we're dating as well. It's kind of bada**,' Megan continued. The midfielder plays for the Seattle Reign FC in Washington and helped the US win gold in the 2012 London Olympics and 2015 FIFA Women's World Cup. 

Sue echo'd her girlfriend's statements about what it means to break down this barrier by appearing as a couple on the cover.

'Yeah, I think it'll probably be even more bada** later. A year, five years, 10 years, 20 years, to look back and be like, "Oh, they were the first openly gay couple to be on the cover, to be in the issue",' she said.  

The basketball player currently plays for the Seattle Storm in Washington and helped the US win four gold medals at the Olympics in 2004, 2008, 2012 and 2016.   

In preparation for the nude photo shoot, Megan and Sue helped each other focus more on nutrition.

'We tightened it up a bit,' Megan said. 'It's not like we fasted or anything. There was a tweak here or there. But I just complained about it the whole time.' 

Sue, as the oldest current player in the WNBA, is the more nutrition-minded individual of the relationship, so she has helped influence Megan to focus more on what she puts in her body. 

'I saw the difference so quickly and saw the change in myself so quickly that I was like, "Oh, this is actually working. I'm gonna keep on this. Keep playin' 'til I'm 37",' Megan said.

Before Sue officially came out to the public in 2017, her family and friends knew she was gay after she come to the realization while attending the University of Connecticut. 

While some thought Megan was the reason Sue officially came out, she said in an interview with ESPN last year that the moment just seemed right. 

'So even though I understand there are people who think I should have done it sooner, it wasn't right for me at the time. I have to be true to that,' she said at the time. 

Sue and Megan met briefly in 2012 but actually connected during the Olympics in 2016 after the US Women's soccer team was eliminated earlier than expected. This gave Megan the opportunity to see more games, including the Women's basketball where Sue was a star on the court. 

Shortly after that, they started dating in the fall of 2016 before going public in 2017.

When asked what is more athletic, soccer or basketball, the couple was diplomatic in their responses.

'Well, we've argued about soccer or basketball - who's the better athlete? Their endurance is something that is pretty insane,' Sue said, 'Just the fact that they can go out there and run, I forget what it is, 8 to 9 miles in an hour and a half.' 

Similarly, Megan praised Sue for her ability to shoot on the fly during a basketball game.

'I've done, like, one shooting drill, and it's so hard. The constant go, go, go,' she said.

While they are both equally competitive on the field and the court, the couple said they lead a relatively chill life when off the clock. 

'It's the most uncompetitive relationship I've ever been in, by far,' Sue said. 'When we're home, it's like you just want to chill.'

Together, they showed off their toned bodies both inside the studio and out in a forest setting for the intimate photo shoot. 

The shoot allowed for them to celebrate not only being the first gay couple on the cover, but also the physical toll athletes go through to perform at the professional level. 

'Just looking through the magazine every year, it's a celebration of athletes in the way that we look, the muscles, the scars,' Megan said. 

Sue added: 'I totally agree. I look at it as more of an honor than anything else.'

But it isn't lost on the athletic stars how much of an influence they have come as a public couple within their fields.

Legendary: Sue is the oldest current player in the WNBA. She was drafted in 2002

Athletic: One thing Sue said that she was impressed by Megan (and soccer players in general) is her endurance level 

Cute: The couple officially met during the 2016 Summer Olympics and have been dating since

'I feel like there are a lot of negative ideas that hopefully I can break down just by being myself,' Megan said. 

Her girlfriend saw a similar approach to helping change the narrative about gay couples.

'For some who maybe didn't know I was gay, I think it meant a lot and it changes some people's perception on what being gay is,' Sue said. 

'I think having a gay couple on [the cover], hopefully it just becomes the norm. You want it to not be an issue. You want it to just be, Oh, another couple is on there.' 

The couple is joined by 14 other athletes for the annual issue - out on newsstands June 29 - including Olympic figure skater Adam Rippon, NBA basketball player Karl-Anthony Towns and soccer player Crystal Dunn.
