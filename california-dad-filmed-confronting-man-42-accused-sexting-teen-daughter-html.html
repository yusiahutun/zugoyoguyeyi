California dad is filmed confronting man accused of sexting daughter

Publish date: 2024-07-14

A California father went undercover to set up a meeting with a 42-year-old man who had been grooming his teenage daughter after finding salacious texts between the pair. 

Todd Thomas told ABC30 he found his daughter, 14, sleeping with a cell phone in her hand one night last year - but he had not allowed her to have one.

Immediately alarmed, the Clovis man picked it up and looked through it, and allegedly found explicit messages from Hugo Rabson.

Mr Thomas and his wife investigated, and found Rabson had given her the phone hidden inside a book after the teenager posted on Whisper asking for someone to send her one. 

Clovis father Todd Thomas confronted a man he claims had been sending explicit messages to his 14-year-old daughter on a phone he gave to her hidden in a book

The defensive father used the phone to set up a meeting in a park between the pair, which was filmed by Mr Thomas' friend. 

Video shows Mr Thomas standing in the background making sure Rabson did not leave before police arrived, while his friend is heard saying: 'the whole world will see you'.

Mr Thomas told KMPH he did not use violence against the man, but wanted to be able to look him in the eye.

The man (left) allegedly had asked her for naked photographs and asked to be called 'Daddy Sugar' (pictured right is Mr Thomas)

'As a father, I had to tell him that I know who you are. I know what you did. And I had to see the monster myself,'

While the 42-year-old continues to shake his head and smile, for just one moment he appears to lose his cool and snarls at the camera. 

Rabson was arrested and charged with 'felony arranging a meeting with a minor for lewd or lascivious behavior'. He pleaded guilty and has been released on a $30,000 bond.  

The texts, seen in an arrest warrant obtained by ABC30, allegedly show Rabson soliciting child pornography and ask her to call him 'Daddy Sugar'. 

'Daddy needs to see his little girl… naked,' one of the texts said.

Mr Thomas told a friend over Facebook Rabson had 'started grooming her to bring a young man to a hotel with her...so he could watch'.

'He would join unless she wanted him too he said',' the father continued.  

Mr Thomas spent a year trying to have Rabson arrested, he claimed, saying his plan was delayed because the 42-year-old was a 'pro'.

Though he went to the park just one day after finding his daughter's contraband phone, detectives needed more evidence to lay charges. 

Court documents obtained by KMPH show officers tracked Rabson's internet records, emails, and looked into his internet and cable providers. They were eventually able to arrest him in January. 

 A spokesman for the sheriff's office said it is easier when parents call law enforcement officials first, as they have more tools at their disposal  

'There are things we can do the general public wouldn't think about,' he said.

Mr Thomas is now setting up a support group called 'Fathership' for others who have been in similar situations.  

He is looking to engage counselors and anyone else with expertise who can help him launch the program.
