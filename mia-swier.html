Mia Swier Biography, Age, Height, Husband, Net Worth

Publish date: 2024-09-24

Mia Swier works for Fox’s production team as a director and producer. She is also the singer and bass guitarist for Guns N’ Hoses, an all-female Guns N’ Roses parody band, and the co-founder of the production business Effin Media.

Early Life

Mia Swier was born on September 1, 1985, to American parents in Alabama, United States of America. She was infatuated with acting as a child, and her idols were Dwayne Jonson and Jennifer Lawrence. She wanted to become a filmmaker because she lacked acting talent. Swier attended a local high school in her neighborhood. She earned a Bachelor’s Degree in Mass Communication and Media Studies from New York University after graduating from high school.

Mia Swier Career

Mia began her career while studying at the Steinhardt School of New York University. She worked in the fields of media studies and television production, as well as for networks such as ABC, NBC, and USA Network. She also spent several years at White Cherry Entertainment, where she was in charge of production for a number of nationally televised events. Mia served as an intern at “Saturday Night Live” after graduating and went on to seek a career in the entertainment industry. In 2009, she and Dan Bricker founded “Effin Media,” a film production firm.

Mia has also been on television series such as The Tony Awards, Who Wants to Be a Millionaire, The NFL Kickoff, The Thanksgiving Day Parade, One Life to Live, Nashville Star, and The Daytime Emmy Awards. In addition, Mia has co-written and produced films such as A (Not So) Civil Union and Somewhere Between. She went on to produce music videos and short films, including Keke Palmer’s “I Don’t Belong to You,” and worked in documentaries such as Californication, House of Lies, Dexter, Homeland, Weeds, Shameless, The Real L Word, Nurse Jackie, and many others.

Von Glitz Production, a Los Angeles-based company, was founded by her in 2012. Mia is a singer and bassist in a rock band. With Garrett Cillo, Alberto Duhau, and Devon Johnson, she formed the alt-punk garage rock band Shoot the Freak in 2009. She formed Guns n’ Hoses, an all-girl GNR tribute band with Charlene Kaye, Erin Marsz, Emmy Wildwood, and Gina Ilasi, after Shoot the Freak broke up in early 2012. She was the lead vocalist in the rock band Mad Moon Riot alongside Matt Hitchens, Ru Hazell, and Pou Piam until March 2015, when she had to leave due to business obligations.
