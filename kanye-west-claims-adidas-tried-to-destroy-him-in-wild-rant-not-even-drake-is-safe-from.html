Kanye West claims Adidas tried to 'destroy him' in wild rant not even Drake is safe from

Publish date: 2024-03-11

Kanye West has yet again thrown swipes at Adidas after he was dropped by the sports brand in October 2022, after the rapper made anti-Semitic remarks.

The music star recently hit number one on the Billboard Hot 100 with his hit, Carnival, and after getting the ‘number one song in the world’ Kanye West is now ‘lashing out’ at the brand he claimed “tried to destroy him”.

Kanye West claims Adidas ‘tried to destroy him’

In a now-deleted video on Instagram, the Touch the Sky rapper wasn’t holding back on his thoughts about the brand, who he created the famous Yeezy’s with.

“Adidas, you tried to destroy me,” the rapper began.

“Now we’ve got the number one song in the world. When I was there, y’all were stealing my ideas and putting them in your company. Then you dismantled my creative team,” the 46-year-old claimed.

He then claimed the company made “fake colorways” and ‘paid off his lawyers’ while he was with them. “Then you dropped me, on a morality clause, even though y’all were the ones stealing my designs.”

In the final bombshell claim, the father of four says the corporation’s CEO told the singer they were going to “burn the product” directly to his face.

The rapper also took swipes via an Instagram caption, which has yet again been deleted.

“F*** Adidas and everybody who works there or with them. Anyone who goes to school with anyone whose parents work at Adidas,” he wrote in part of the lengthy rant.

GRV Media and Celebrity Tidbit have contacted Adidas for comment.

The rapper also takes a swipe at Drake

Adidas wasn’t the only one in the firing line. Ye also reignited the feud with fellow rapper Drake.

In the lengthy deleted Insta caption, Ye penned: “And it’s f*** Drake for taking Durk right at the beginning of the Vultures role out.”

The God’s Planner rapper recently bought Lil Durk to some of the final dates of his It’s All a Blur – Big as the What? tour, who appears on Ye and Ty Dolla $ign’s title track in Vultures.

The swipe at Drake comes as no surprise as the pair’s complicated relationship goes back 15 years. No response from the Toronto-born rapper yet, but Ye has warned that if anyone else comes to his head, he’ll be back.

So expect more drama!
