Publish date: 2024-04-26

Brooke Shields is working on a new book.

The actress, 58, who took her new puppy, Tuzi, with her to a 5k fundraiser, will tackle the topic of middle age, according to People. 

Publisher Flatiron Books told the outlet the tome will 'candidly explore both the humility and power of aging.'

Editor Julie Will said in a statement: 'Brooke Shields has been a leader, an advocate, and a role model for generations of women.

'Now, as those of us who grew up with her navigate the uncertainties—and opportunities—of midlife, we can look forward to her relatable perspective once more.'

New book: Brooke Shields, 58, will tackle middle age in a new book which will explore 'the humility and power of aging' (pictured in New York in April)

Brooke said that she and co-author Rachel Bertsche want to flip the popular beliefs about aging. 

The publication will blend the authors' personal experience with guidance for adjusting to the next chapter in readers' lives. 

'What we know for sure about women and aging is that it’s not the put-her-out-to-pasture narrative our society would have us believe,' the People's Choice Award winner explained. 

'I don’t need to hear any more about all the ways my body starts to sag when my ovaries stop working.'

The actress and producer, who has branched out into podcasting and has founded the company Beginning is Now, has a lot of questions she wants answered about the aging process.

'I want to know why I suddenly feel this surge of power? Why am I finally emboldened to try new things?' she queried

'Why does our culture treat women "of a certain age" like they’re past their prime, while so many other cultures revere their elders?' she continued.

'How can we approach this time as the new beginning it really is, rather than the beginning of the end?'

Brooke has already written the Down Came the Rain: My Journey Through Postpartum Depression, is 2006.

In 2015, she examined her often turbulent relationship with her mother, who suffered from alcoholism 'There Was a Little Girl.'

The former child star also opened up about her how she came to feel her own power after being sexualized in her early career in the Hulu documentary Pretty Baby: Brooke Shields.  The film has been nominated for two Prime Time Emmys.

Brooke shared the big news on social media writing, 'Coming soon to a shelf near you.'

A release date has not been announced.
