UPDATE: New details in gunfire incident on 12th Street in Davenport

Publish date: 2024-01-30

UPDATE: The Davenport Police Department have released new details on the incident on Perry Street this morning.

According to a news release, Davenport police, fire and Medic EMS were called to the 100 block of East 12th Street for a report of shots fired with a victim. When they arrived, officers found a man with apparent gunshot wounds. He was taken to a hospital by Medic EMS for treatment of serious, life-threatening injuries. This does not appear to be a random act of violence, according to the release.

Anyone with information about the incident should call the Davenport Police Department at (563) 326-6125, click here to report a tip anonymously or use the P3 Tips app.

This incident remains under investigation and no further information is currently available.

EARLIER: There is a heavy police presence in Davenport this morning after a report of an incident in the 1200 block of Perry Street a little after 4 a.m. today. The block has been sectioned off with crime scene tape and police and investigators were still on the scene at 11 a.m. One person was transported from the scene, Davenport Police told an Our Quad Cities News crew.

Details are limited and Our Quad Cities News has reached out to the Davenport Police Department for more information.

This is a breaking story and we will have more details as they become available.
