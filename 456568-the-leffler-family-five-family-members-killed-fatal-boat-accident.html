The Leffler Family: Four Family Members Killed in Fatal Boat Accident

Publish date: 2024-03-06
  • Chris, Lori, Zack and Nate Leffler died suddenly on May 28, 2022, in a tragic boating accident on the Wilmington River
  • The Leffler's daughter Katie was the only family member to survive the crash. further information indicated the family had moved to the area recently
  • A memorial service for the Leffler family will be held on Saturday, June 4, 2022, at Calvary Baptist Temple in Savannah

A Savannah family in the United States (US) has lost four people in a boat crash that happened on the Wilmington River on Saturday, May 28.

The accident happened after two boats coming from opposite directions slammed into each other, killing five people and injuring four more.

Four family members die in an accident

A report by WSBTV showed Chris Leffler, who was a history teacher at a Christian school in Savannah, was killed along with his wife, Lori, and his two sons, Zack and Nate.

Investigators arrested 45-year-old Mark Stegall of Savannah for boating under the influence in relation to this incident, but officials have not released an official cause of the crash.

The Lefflers' daughter, Katie, and a friend survived the crash.

A man on the other boat, 37-year-old Robert Chauncey, was also killed. In addition, that boat's driver, 45-year-old Mark Stegall, was arrested for boating under the influence.

Further reports indicated the Leffler family had just moved to Savannah a few months ago.

Chris Leffler described as a hardworking teacher

Edward Spurka, the head of the school where Chris taught before moving to Savanna, said the shocking news took the wind out of their entire community.

Spurka said Chris was always first to arrive and last to leave.

“He loved our school. He loved our kids. He was a father figure to every student,” Spurka said.

He said the teacher loved teaching so much that he recently took a group of students to Italy in January 2022.

Worshippers gathered at the Calvary Baptist Temple to remember the four members of the Leffler family who died during the Memorial Day weekend.

Lori received her BS from Bucknell University and her masters from Duquesne University.

She began her career at PPG Industries, and her hard work led to her current position as Vice President of Environmental Health and Safety at KIK Consumer Products.

Woman loses 3 children in road accident

In a related story, TUKO.co.ke reported a woman identified as Miriam Nansubuga from Iganga district in eastern Uganda was mourning the sudden death of her three daughters following a tragic bus accident.

The 30-year-old woman was travelling with her daughters on Wednesday, May 4, using a bus belonging to the Link Bus when the accident snuffed out the young lives of the three girls.
