Monte Morris can do almost everything inside a basketball court, but he too feels weary when he sees a kid without a father figure in their life.

Witnessing such a situation in the lives of people, emotion starts getting kicked into the point guard as he remembers his past.

Like many who did not get the love and guidance of their father, Monte also grew up with the care of a dad.

His father passed away when he was tiny, and the youngster feels the same emotion when he sees someone going through that pain.

Parents, Height & College Info

Monte Morris was born in the city of Grand Rapids, Miami, to mother Layonia Morris and dad, who passed away due to a heart attack when he was just an infant. 

He celebrates his birthday on 27 June every year and lives with his single mother in a family of two. As the only man in the house, life was very different for the Denver Nuggets point guard.

Monte Morris with mother Layonia Morris in November 2019 (Photo: Monte Morris's Instagram)

He trained with his mother form a young age as she was the basketball coach of his high school. Since then, the 24-year-old was always around basketball. 

In no time, Monte started showing off good signs of improvement in his game. The quarterback was named Mr.Basketball and standout player at Flint Beecher High School in 2013.

After that, he joined to play for the Iowa State University Cyclones in the class of 2017. There, he made playing look easy from his ridiculous assist to turnover ratio. In the final years in the college competition NCAA, the guard set the new record of assist to turnover ratio of 5.21.

Finally, at the end of 2017, the player of height 6 feet 2 inches (1.88m) opted to a two-way rookie deal with the Denver Nuggets in the NBA.

Now, in his third year with the team, Monte has already developed as a tremendous player playing against the likes of LeBron James, Stephen Curry, and James Hayden. He is sure to grab a big deal in the future with his consistent performances in the NBA.
