Who is Hailey Zenk?

Hailey Zenk is a Lincoln County teenager who was charged with the death of her three friends after a car accident. The friends were headed home after a night of drinking and smoking hemp when Zenk's 2008 Ford Focus flew off the track and ploughed straight into a tree head-on. 

Hailey will now be tried as an adult liable for the vehicle's steering after the denial of her reprieve to be tried as a minor, as she was 17 at the time of the accident.

What happened to Hailey Zenk?

The prosecution in the teen's car accident case finally managed to get their request and have her tried as an adult for the Missouri accident that resulted in the deaths of her friends. The car accident tragically ended the lives of three teenagers on the morning of February 5, 2023.

The defendant was allegedly drunk at the time of the crash, and after her arrest, the first court hearing was set for May 2023. The arraignment was then pushed to August 2023 to give the prosecution more time to build up a case against her.

After a long wait, a Lincoln judge finally accepted the prosecution's plea to charge her as an adult under state law in the August ruling. The decision was reinstated because Hailey had turned 18 and could no longer get lenient judgments under juvenile prosecution statutes.

The victim's parents, including Kaeden Tyler's stepdad, Douglas Meyers, had long requested the ruling despite strong opposition from Hailey Zenk's family. The plaintiff's families retaliated against the fact that Hailey got back home to be with her family while their children paid with their lives for her mistakes. 

The defendant was 17 years old at the time of the crash, and Hailey Zenk's parents were deeply remorseful but opposed the ruling, requesting a juvenile trial. At the time of the ruling, the defendant was placed under house arrest and strapped with a leg monitor as part of her bail conditions.

Hailey Zenk's car accident

On the fateful morning of February 5, 2023, Zenk hopped into a 2008 Ford Focus with her friends. However, a few minutes into their drive, the teens' journey would soon turn tragic, as they were involved in a car crash around 4 a.m.

Zenk was at wheels when the car skidded into a tree and overturned near Moscow Mills, Ethington Road. William Flickinger, Emily McNees, and Kaeden Tyler were pronounced dead by paramedics, who later arrived at the scene.

The defendant and the other surviving passenger, Trevor J. Bogert, were rushed to Mercy Hospital. At the hospital, she was kept under round-the-clock supervision before she was taken into custody for her booking for the alleged murder charges.

Hailey was later released and put under house arrest with a GPS-guided leg monitoring device closely checking her every movement as she awaited trial.

The deceased were all students who schooled at St. Charles Community College, William at New Horizons High School, and the youngest, a ninth-grade student, Kaeden, enrolled in Grade Centre in Moscow Mills.

Hailey Zenk's charges

Hailey's charges were upgraded from murder to facing a series of five felony charges that included one count of DWI resulting in the death of two, one count of DWI resulting in serious injury, and three counts of first-degree involuntary murder.
