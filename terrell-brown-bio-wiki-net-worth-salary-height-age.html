Terrell Brown began his career as a tape editor at WWBT-TV, NBC12 News. Later, he joined CBS News in 2009 as a correspondent, where he anchored Up to the Minute. Moreover, he covered the Boston Marathon bombing, Superstorm Sandy and many more.

Furthermore, Terrell was awarded the 2014 national Emmy Award for his work on the Boston Marathon bombing suspects and the 48 HOURS special report detailing the manhunt. Similarly, he won the Los Angeles Area Emmy Award. Following this, he worked as an anchor/reporter at WMBT-TV for four years.
