Nairobi Arboretum entrance fees, location, activities, and rules

Publish date: 2024-02-18

Located in the south-central part of Kenya is Nairobi County. The county serves as the capital city of Kenya. It is a home to different tourist attractions and over 4 million residents with a mixture of all the 42 Kenyan tribes. Walking 3 kilometres from the Nairobi central business is the primary recreational centre, the Nairobi Arboretum.

Nairobi Arboretum is a perfect site for a person seeking rest from the city's hustle. It was established in 1907 by Batiscombe, the Deputy Conservator of Forests then. Later on, it became a national reserve through a gazette notice issued by the government in 1932.

The public has found it to be a unique place for recreational activities. It is a green space in the city's centre that several residents, school children and tourists find refreshing. In addition, it is easily accessible by foot from the city and very affordable.

What is found in Nairobi Arboretum?

This welcoming garden is home to various plants and animal species. It has more than 350 species of indigenous and exotic plants. Additionally, it hosts over 100 species of birds and different species of monkeys. Once you've arrived at Nairobi Arboretum, you'll have a variety of things to explore, thanks to the dry mountain forest vegetation.

1. Trees and biodiversity

The Arboretum has developed through time into a crucial green oasis. It boasts of more than 350 tree species. These include broad-leaved drought-deciduous trees, shrubs and other exotic trees. The trees are randomly planted with the woody shrubs scattered here and there. The mixture includes bamboo trees and other samples from various parts of the world.

2. Birds

Nairobi Arboretum has over 100 resident and migratory bird species such as Bronze Sunbird, White-eyed Slaty Flycatcher, African pied wagtail, Baglafecht weaver and Montane White-eye. Occasionally, you would spot the Common bulbul with its brown feather and white underpants pocking on grass.

3. Reptiles, animals and insects

There are also a variety of insects, reptiles, and tiny animals, including the amusing Velvet and Sykes monkeys which illustrate our rich natural and cultural heritage. Among the reptiles are Jackson's three-horned chameleon and High-casqued chameleon.

4. Butterflies

It is common to find butterflies in Nairobi Arboretum. Visiting Arboretum gives you a chance to see the beautiful Golden Piper butterfly. It is a distinct species known for its black and orange colour.

The other species include the Green-banded swallowtail and Green-veined Charaxes butterflies. In addition, the presence of fruits and leftovers from visitors has attracted ants, snails, termites, and beetles.

Nairobi Arboretum activities

The famous tourist attraction was set aside expressly for the study of trees and vegetation. However, there are various things to do in Arboretum, both as an individual or a group. This is a good place for meditating, running and doing yoga. Tourists can also stop by after travelling from one place to another to enjoy the fresh air and revitalize and enjoy nature's quiet before they continue with their journey.

How much does it cost to enter the Arboretum?

Nairobi Arboretum fees vary depending on age and the purpose behind your visit. For recreational activities and site-seeing, the entry fee for adults is Ksh. 65 while that of a child is Ksh.27. However, the fees for commercial purposes vary from one activity to another.

For instance, the fee for photography is Ksh. 1,322 per camera, while for videography, one has to pay Ksh. 3,306 per camera. For events such as workshops or weddings, the ground hire is about Ksh. 50,000. The fee can only be paid via Mpesa or a card at the gate.

It is also good to note that the Arboretum is a significant place that does not have different charges for Kenyan citizens and non-citizens.

Is food allowed in Nairobi Arboretum?

Yes, food and soft drinks are allowed in Nairobi Arboretum. There is also a restaurant at the main entrance where one can buy food. However, alcohol and alcoholic drinks are not allowed within the park. It is also good to take note of the following when visiting Nairobi Arboretum:

  • Do not feed wild animals.
  • Take all the litter that you brought away with you.
  • Enjoy yourself; in addition to seeing, pause and listen regularly.
  • Do not start fires in the forest.
  • Do not take away animals, animal products, plants or plant parts.
  • Avoid unnecessary noise as it disturbs both neighbours and other visitors.
  • Do not mark or deface tree stems, stones and other features.
  • Keep to the designated tracks and paths when walking in the forest.

Contacts and location

Nairobi Arboretum is situated along state house road in Kilimani, Nairobi, Kenya. It has two entrances. The main entrance is located southeast of the Arboretum. It is characterized by a large car park and a few stalls that sell snacks and refreshments. Next to the entrance is a small restaurant and a toilet.

The second entrance is located next to the intersection of Arboretum Drive and Ring Road Kilileshwa. It has a small ticket office. The park is under the management of the Kenya Forest Service and Friends of Nairobi Arboretum.
