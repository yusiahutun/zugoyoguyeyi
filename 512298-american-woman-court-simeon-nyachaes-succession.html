Simeon Nyachae's 4th Wife In Vicious Succession Battle With Late Minister's Wife, Stepson

Publish date: 2024-02-06
  • Margaret Nyachae Chweya is accusing co-wife Grace Wamuyu and her stepson Leon Nyachae of attempting to lock her out of the succession of late minister Simeon Nyachae
  • Margaret, 66, who lives in Los Angeles in the US said that she was married to the former powerful KANU minister through a traditional wedding and were blessed with three kids
  • She accuses Grace and Leon of attempting to alter the will signed by Nyachae in their bid to dispossess her of her rightful
  • Margaret said that she fell in love with Nyachae when she worked as a civil servant at Jogoo House and that the late minister bought her a house in Loresho

An American woman claiming to be the fourth wife of the late Cabinet Minister Simeon Nyachae has sued her co-wife Grace Wamuyu and her son over the control of his multi-billion estate.

In court papers seen by TUKO.co.ke, Margaret Nyachae Chweya has named Wamuyu and Leon Nyachae as the respondents claiming that the two have conspired to strike out her family from a willl and keep her from inheriting her late husband's vast estate.

The woman told the court that she fell in love with Nyachae at Jogoo House and as far she was concerned, all her co-wives know her.

"I am known to my co-wives, Martha and Grace Wamuyu, unless things have changed. From my end I know all my co-wives and I hold the position that all wives must be treated equally," she said.

Through the law firm of Mogaka Musyoki and Company Advocates, Chweya reiterated that she was not just claiming to be Nyachae's wife as she was married to the late minister in a traditional wedding in 1970.

"That I am Mrs Simeon Nyachae the fourth wife. I am not claiming like Leon states. I got married traditionally to Hon. Simeon Nyachae before Leon Nyachae was born, and that is also before Grace Wamuyu Leon's mother was known to Hon Simeon Nyachae. Nyachae paid my dowry to my late uncle Samson Obaga Okao because my dad David Chweya died on July 3, 1970," Chweya said in court papers.

The 66-year-old woman who is a resident of Los Angeles in the United States of America, disclosed that the late minister bought her a house in Loresho as his wife.

"Nyachae married his wives traditionally. All wives are considered equal. I am Honorable Simeon Nyachae's wife and I have his children. I am not a lesser person in regards to the position I held in his life. He loved me as his wife, he bought me a home for me and my children," the papers stated.

Simeon Nyachae's properties

In her affidavit, she has denied ever being married to four other men as claimed by her stepson Leon.

"I have never been legally married four times. George Gordon Odero has neither been my boyfriend nor my husband," Chweya added.

The woman said that she has three children from her union with Nyachae and rubbished findings by a private investigator that Leon presented to the court, terming the evidence as misleading, incomplete and intentionally altered to besmirch her.

Chweya is contesting the authenticity of a will presented in court claiming that the signature is different from that of Nyachae.

"The signature on the copy of the presented will is different from that of my husband. I am contesting the authenticity of the only thing they have presented to this court as a will. The said executors conflict the content of the said will which leaves out some of the properties owned by my late husband," she added.

Simeon Nyachae's children

Chweya alleged that Wamuyu and her step son had already sold some of Nyachae's properties, insisting that hers was a battle of wives and not children.

"That Leon and his mother Grace Wamuyu and the rest of the respondents have sold some of my husband's properties to American investors without minding that succession is in progress. This is a battle of wives not children," she stated.

It is her contention that the respondents have not produced the original will as the standard procedure dictates.

Chweya who was working as a civil servant in the 70s, claims that, what her stepchildren had done was to erase Nyachae's beneficiaries like her American based family and sold some of the properties in contention.

She argued that it was Nyachae who relocated her to America to protect her and children from his wife. The matter will be heard on 4 the July.
