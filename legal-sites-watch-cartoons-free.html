The 7 Best Places to Watch Cartoons Online for Free (Legally)

Publish date: 2024-03-03

Many people grew up watching cartoons. Either ones that run in the background, and you don't pay much attention to them, or ones you've waited to see all week and completely engross you.

Cartoons have been an integral part of many childhoods. So much so that there's no longer a need for people to be children to enjoy them. These animated features have become nostalgic and comforting for many.

If you're craving some good old cartoons are some of the best websites to consume cartoons legally and for free.

1. SuperCartoons

SuperCartoons is another legal platform that lets you watch animated features for free. It offers a catalog of over a thousand classic cartoons you can enjoy, and who doesn't love watching those?

You can find various Disney or Looney Tunes animations, both old and new. And you can browse through the site's convenient sections to find what you feel like watching. There are Series, Characters, Cartoons, and even Studio sections. The latter lets you browse cartoons by Walt Disney, Warner Bros, Hanna-Barbera, and MGM, among others. It's super convenient to navigate.

2. Nickelodeon

Nickelodeon is among the most well-known entertainment platforms. It offers an extensive catalog of animated and live-action features and has a healthy mix of old and new cartoons, so you can dive into nostalgia or discover something new to consume.

The best part about the streaming platform is that you can enjoy its content for free. It's also completely legal.

3. Cartoon Network

Cartoon Network is another well-known platform. It's been around for a while and has established itself as one of the best places to watch animated features.

You can find all of the 90s and early 2000s nostalgia shows, like The Powerpuff Girls and Ben 10, alongside some newer titles like The Amazing World of Gumball and Craig of the Creek. The platform is free and legal, so you can dive right in and enjoy.

4. Boomerang

Boomerang is chock-full of old and new cartoon titles you can browse. On the platform, you can find The Flintstones, The Jetsons, Scooby-Doo, Tom and Jerry, Popeye, and even The Smurfs. And these are only a small batch of titles available.

Boomerang's cartoon library is quite extensive, and the platform is entirely legal and mostly free. Let's elaborate.

If you want to watch features on Boomerang, you can start a one-week free trial and decide if it's worth the subscription. After your free trial period ends, a plan will cost you $5.99/month or $49.99/year, which is hardly a bad price.

If you, however, feel like you have too many streaming services and don't want to spend money on more, then you can stick to the free cartoons available on the platform. Yes, Boomerang offers a dedicated Free Episodes section called Saturday Morning, and you can watch all of them without financial commitment. It's also an ad-free platform, which is always a benefit.

5. WB Kids

WB Kids is the official YouTube channel for Warner Bros. content for kids. It's full of cartoons you can browse, and everything you see on the channel is licensed and, therefore, free.

You'll find a ton of videos on Looney Tunes, Scooby-Doo, and Tom & Jerry. Some of the videos are shorter than others, as they tend to range between three-minute clips to around twenty-minute ones.

You can also keep up with new Warner Bros. projects as the channel often posts trailers among the cartoons.

6. DC Kids

DC Kids is an official YouTube channel, meaning you can watch it legally. And, since it's on YouTube, you can also enjoy it for free.

As the name lets on, the channel offers everything DC-related, including series like Teen Titans Go! and all the major superheroes. You can catch Batman, Superman, and Wonder Woman in a variety of shows.

DC Kids has all manner of cartoons available. Granted, they gear towards kids, so the humor and scenes won't be explicit or graphic, but neither is SpongeBob, and it's still a staple for Saturday mornings. And if you prefer adult animated shows, you can always try Netflix or any of the other streaming services since they offer plenty of such features.

7. SpongeBob SquarePants Official

Speaking of SpongeBob, it has an official YouTube channel, too. It uploads content every day from Thursday to Sunday, and every video is SpongeBob-related.

The YouTube channel doesn't only feature beloved scenes from the cartoon, which you can also catch on the official Nickelodeon channel, but offers up new content with the titular character, too. Every video available is licensed and free to watch.

Don’t Resist the Nostalgia of Cartoons

Nostalgia will always have some sort of pull on people, whether it's with old movies, clothes, or cartoons. Whether you grew up with the classic Cartoon Network roster, the Disney Channel flicks that came after, or they're something you've begun to enjoy more recently, it doesn't really matter.

Regardless of age, cartoons tend to instill a sense of nostalgia in those watching. So, playing and enjoying them, absentmindedly or not, is comforting. Not to mention, you get to keep up with all the SpongeBob memes always going around.

So, give into the nostalgia, and don't deny yourself the comfort of playing a cartoon from your childhood simply because you're no longer a child.
