Meghan Markle's 'lost potential' is clear in new photo, says expert

Publish date: 2024-09-21

Meghan Markle left the royal family in 2020, and it looks like Queen Camilla Consort has taken her place. She’s currently flying the flag for the family at a range of Women’s Charities, while the Duchess lives her life away from the institution in the States.

Before getting married to Prince Harry, as well as being an actor, Meghan Markle was an avid activist for women’s rights, even giving a speech at a UN Women’s conference. This week, Queen Consort Camilla was joined by a group of young campaigners from the charity SafeLives, which one expert says should have been Meghan’s rule in the royal family…

‘This should be Meghan’

After the event, the Queen posed with four of the organization’s young Changemakers. “Looking at it, the very first thing I thought was, this should be Meghan,” royal expert Daniela Elser wrote for NewsAU.

She goes on to say “a member of the royal family welcoming young women fighting to tackle violence against women into the palace and advocating for their work,” is actually what anyone would have predicted for the Duchess when she joined in 2018.

However, it all went downhill in 2020, when the couple up and left, and that’s when Camilla, stepped up. In 2020, she became a patron of SaveLives and has also recently hosted a reception at the palace to highlight the U.N.’s annual 16 Days of Activism against Gender-Based Violence, just to name a few.

“The Queen is the unsung feminist hero we never knew we needed or actually, you know, had,” Elser writes. Although she says it’s ‘hard to wrap your head around,’ she states Camilla has become “a loud, proud voice for women and girls.”

According to the expert, Meghan had “So much promise, so much good intent, so much sound and fury which has so far not exactly signified all that much.”

Although she still does work with the Archewell Foundation, Daniela writes, “I don’t mean to diminish this all as it is valuable, good work, but it’s a question of scale, convening power, and follow-through.”
