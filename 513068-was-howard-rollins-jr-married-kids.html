Who was Howard Rollins?

Howard Rollins was a stage, television and film actor. He was born on October 17, 1950, and died on December 8, 1996. Who was part of Howard Rollins’ family? The actor was born to Ruth Rollins and steelworker Howard Ellsworth Sr. (died in 1980). He was the youngest of four children.

Rollins attended Northern High School and graduated in 1968. He later enrolled at Townson State University, where he studied theatre.

Rollin left college in 1970 and immediately got into the entertainment industry. One of his first roles was in the PBS soap opera Our Street, where he played Slick. Howard later moved to New York, where he got other roles, including the Broadway productions of We Interrupt This Program (1975), The Mighty Gents (1978) and G.R Point (1979).

Howard appeared in numerous other movies, TV shows and stage productions over the years, and some of the most popular include Virgil Tibbs and In the Heat. In the Heat started airing on NBC in 1988. Unfortunately, during the show’s running, the actor struggled with drug addiction and alcohol-related crimes, spending one month in jail for reckless driving and driving under the influence.
