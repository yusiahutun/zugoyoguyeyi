Parents of brothers accused of killing neighbor found dead

Publish date: 2024-01-26

The parents of two brothers accused of beating to death an elderly neighbor were discovered dead inside their Illinois home Sunday in what officials have described as an apparent murder-suicide.

Patrick Morgan, 49, and his wife Angel Morgan, 42, were found unresponsive at around 4am Sunday at their home in the 400 block of Bluff Street in Joliet and were pronounced dead at the scene, according to the Will County Coroner's Office.

The deceased couple were the parents of Blaique Morgan, 20, and his 18-year-old brother, Amari, who were arrested last year and charged with murder in the beating death of their next-door neighbor, 62-year-old Robert Bielec.

Violent death: Angel and Patrick Morgan, pictured as a young couple, were found dead in an apparent murder-suicide Sunday, more than a year after their two sons were arrested

According to police, on the night of January 7, 2016, Bielec had pulled into his driveway on Houston Avenue when the Morgan brothers confronted the man about what officials have described as an 'ongoing dispute' between the two families.

Assistant State's Attorney John Connor said that during the argument that ensued, Bielec made a sexual comment about the Morgans’ younger sister, prompting then-17-year-old Amari to smash the man with a baseball bat in the head.

Connor said that impact caused the victim’s skull to 'cave in,' killing him on the spot.

An autopsy later found the cause of death to have been blunt force trauma and the coroner ruled the manner of death as a homicide, leading to the arrests of Blaique and Amari Morgan.

A little more than a year later, the siblings' parents were discovered shot dead early Sunday morning by a relative who came by their apartment to perform a welfare check, having failed to reach them all day, police said.

After finding the husband and wife deceased, the family member left their home and called the police. Officers who responded to the scene found a handgun near the bodies, reported Patch Joliet.

Angel and Patrick Morgan had been together for 21 years, but court records suggest that theirs was a troubled marriage: the wife obtained an order of protection against the husband in 2009 that lasted for three weeks, according to the Chicago Tribune.

Victim: Robert Bielec, 62, a US Army veteran and widower, died from a blunt force trauma to the head at the scene   

Then in May of last year, Mrs Morgan filed for divorce but then stopped the proceedings four months ago.

Amari and Blaique Morgan are currently being held at the Will County Jail on $1.5million bond each.

During their bond hearing last year, The Tribune reported that the Morgans’ younger sister stood up in court and proclaimed their innocence. Angel Morgan also attended the hearing.

According to his obituary, Robert Bielec was a veteran of the US Army and a widower who worked as a painter and loved riding his Harley Davidson bike. He lived in Joliet with his two dogs, Bo and Floppy, and a cat.

'He was a caring man who would do anything for anyone,' the obituary read.
