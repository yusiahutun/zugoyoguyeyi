Chick-fil-A replaces In-N-Out as the nation's favorite fast food restaurant in satisfaction survey

Publish date: 2024-07-29

America has a new favorite fast-food restaurant, and it doesn't do burgers.

Chick-fil-A unseated In-N-Out as the no. 1 fast-food restaurant in the latest Market Force annual satisfaction survey. 

More than 7,600 customers were polled in the survey, which takes into account quality of food, healthy food options, speed of service, value, cleanliness, friendliness of staff, curb appeal and atmosphere.  

Chicken sandwich chain Chick-fil-A, based in College Park, Georgia, got a 79 percent score on the index. 

The 2018 leader In-N-Out - based in Irvine, California - only managed 73 percent in the this year's report, falling  to third place. 

Raising Cane, another chicken fast-food chain, came second, with 78% composite loyalty index score.

The survey also gave Chick-fil-A staff a score of 82 percent for staff friendliness over In-N-Out's 73%. Chick-fil-A also scored 82% for overall cleanliness, higher than any other chain. 

Founded in 1946 by S. Truett Cathy, a devout Southern Baptist, Chick-fil-A has closed on Sundays, Thanksgiving and Christmas, but has still grown to become the nation's largest chicken fast food restaurant operator with about 2,500 locations.

Despite controversial remarks in opposition to same-sex marriage made by Cathy's son Dan, Chick-fil-A's chief executive, the company has remained a strong favorite among fast food junkies.

In-N-Out ranked number 1 overall in last year's Market Force customer satisfaction survey

'Since Truett Cathy opened his first restaurant in 1946, we have served quality food, made with care,' writes the company on its website. 'We still carry with us this tremendous sense of responsibility and believe in being a force for good – especially when it comes to our chicken.'

In-N-Out was founded in 1948 by Harry and Esther Snyder in Baldwin Park, California. 

The chain, which operates restaurants along the Pacific Coast and in the Southwest, built its reputation on serving burgers, fries and soft drinks 'in a friendly, sparkling-clean atmosphere,' says the company on its website.
