Horror as man tries to plunge Mitsubishi off Humber Bridge at over 100mph

Publish date: 2024-03-20

Footage shows Jonathon Dent driving his Mitsubishi L200 4x4 at dangerously high speeds over the bridge – near Kingston upon Hull, between Yorkshire and North Lincolnshire – before swerving into the barriers.

Dent's car was left in a mangled heap after being catapulted over the side but narrowly missed falling into the water below, in the incident on July 3, 2016.

The 32-year-old had to be cut out of the car and suffered serious injuries including a broken arm, but amazingly survived.

The crash caused £11,000 worth of damage to the bridge and put members of the public at “severe risk”.

Dent was sentenced to three months in prison, suspended for two years and disqualified for driving for three years at Grimsby Crown Court last week (January 20).

The court heard how Dent – who was travelling at speeds of between 95 and 104mph at the time of the collision – had been having personal issues.

Stephen Welch, prosecuting, said: “He said he was at home and had been having issues with the Child Support Agency, an ex-girlfriend and his partner.

“His lorry was parked in Immingham and he intended to go there and sleep in a cab.”

But he said the defendant had decided to head towards the Humber Bridge in an attempt to calm down.

When asked by police why he had driven into the barriers, Dent said: "I don't know, I had probably just had enough."

Paul Genney, mitigating, said it was the first time he had been in trouble.

“There could have been people on the walkway and there were other cars that had to witness the incident,” he added.

“He wanted to kill himself, he was desperate. It opened his eyes to problems eh was not aware of then and he has made steps to address that.

Dent, of Ainslie Street in Grimsby, admitted dangerous driving.
