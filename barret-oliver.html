Who exactly is Barret Oliver?

Barret Oliver is a former American actor best known for his role as Bastian Balthazar Bux in the film adaptation of the renowned novel ‘The Neverending Story.’ In addition, he received the Saturn Award in 1986 for his work in D.A.R.Y.L.

He is currently employed as a professional photographer. He also wrote the book A History of the Woodburytype.

Professional Life, Career

His first encounter in front of the camera, at such a young age, was in the 1980s, when he appeared in a commercial for ‘Jell-O,’ alongside actor Bill Cosby. Following that, he had a brief role as Jimmy the Kid in the television series The Incredible Hulk, and he later acted in the film ‘The Circle Family’ in 1982, followed by another film ‘Jekyll and Hyde… Together Again’ the same year.

Barret was picked from among 200 children who auditioned for the cinematic adaptation of the best-selling novel ‘The Neverending Story.’ After that, there was no going back, and he has amassed over 20 film credits to date. Some of his most prominent roles include Knight Rider in 1982, American Fantasy Drama Tv Series Highway to Heaven in 1984, and D.A.R.Y.L, The Twilight Zone, The Neverending Story, and The Circle Family, among others.

He quit acting in 1989 to pursue a career as a photographer and printer. He specializes in nineteenth-century photographic methods such as collodion and Woodburytype. Furthermore, his images have been shown in museums and gallery shows, as well as in films. Oliver is also the author of A History of the Woodburytype, which was released in 2007 by Carl Mautz Publishing.
