Kamba names and their meanings

Just like any other tribe in Kenya, the giving of names to children is an important phase of the Akamba people. In most situations, the first four children, get their names after both the maternal and paternal grandparents of the family.The first boy is called after the paternal grandfather and the second after the maternal grandfather.Girls are also given names in the same manner. Due to the respect that these people have between the different relationships, there are people they cannot relate with on “first name” terms.

It is forbidden for the father and the mother in-law on the husband’s side, to call their for instance, daughter in-law by her first name. She can also never call them by their first names. However, when she gets children she has name them after the grandparents. The solution to this problem was to name the children names that described the quality or career of the grandparents. Therefore, when a lady is married into a certain family, she gets a family name, of which she must give her first son.
