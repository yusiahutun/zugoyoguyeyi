List of Satellite internet providers in Kenya to choose from

Publish date: 2024-07-06

Kenya's digital space is one of the emerging and fast-growing sectors of the economy. But is satellite internet available in Kenya? Yes, satellite internet is available in Kenya, and numerous internet provider firms have sprung up to quench the demand for network connections. The advantage of satellite internet is its ability to serve even the remotest areas. What are the country's best satellite internet providers, and what are their price packages?

One key advantage of a satellite internet connection is its ability to transmit wirelessly without optic cables. Once a dish is mounted, it can receive data from the orbiting satellites and is cost-effective, a return for investment in the long run.

Satellite internet providers in Kenya

Kenya has embraced technology rapidly compared to other African countries. From tech firms to young aspiring entrepreneurs, all these categories rely on the digital space to create jobs and earn revenue. Here is a list of satellite internet providers in Kenya:

1. Vizocom Satelite Internet and VSAT Solutions

Vizocom is a dish satellite internet provider that offers wireless internet services to key governmental institutions in the mining, construction, oil, and gas industries.

The firm relies on its Ku-Band, C-Band, and Ka-Band to offer a reliable and flexible range of internet services to its clientele at a sustainable fee that will greatly reduce the cost of the operations.

Vizocom is a reliable internet provider with a presence in the United States and Europe, and its tech support services include Telstar 11N, Africasat 1A, ABS-2, Arabsat 5C, and NSS-12. The company also offers ICT & and integrated security services and options such as voice IP solutions.

2. SKYNET- TELKOM

Telkom runs its SKYNET program from KES 4,999 monthly with packages of 20GB and speeds of 20mbps for small firms. SkyNet runs on the Ka-Band satellite broadband services, which run on the 26.5 to 40GHz frequencies.

SkyNet also offers high-end satellite services for large and multinational companies through its SkyNet Lite Power 100 and Lite Power 200 packages. At KES 19,339.64 and KES 30.943.42, data volumes run at 100GB/10 Mbps to 2000GB/15 Mbps for the Lite Power 100 and 200, respectively.

3. NTvsat

NTvsat is a German internet provider that offers satellite internet solutions to numerous firms in Kenya. Through its satellite broadband connection, NTvsat offers ideal internet solutions to enable remote areas to get access without a wireless connection. 

NTvsat connects its customers through the use of IP Trunking, which is useful for businesses and interlinks to the internet through the use of satellite dishes. The internet provider's services range from 10Mbps to more, and the price is available upon request on its website.

4. Starlink satellite internet

Starlink is one of the best satellite internet services owned by billionaire American entrepreneur Elon Musk. The Satellite provider has already proved crucial in the Russo-Ukrainian war by providing troops with real-time data to front-line commanders to pound enemy positions.

The American firm already has a foothold in Africa with its presence in African countries such as Mozambique, Nigeria, and Rwanda. Earlier in 2023, the company announced its venture into Kenya and offered internet packages at KES 6,500 per month. 

Users must pay around KES 89,000 before installation and a further Ksh 3,100 as shipping fees for Starlink hardware. Starlink Satellite service is suitable for running internet connections for large firms with speeds of up to 150Mbps.

5. Viasat satellite internet

Viasat Satellite Internet is among the best and most reliable mobile satellite internet companies that offer reliable home satellites. The internet service is delivered through small antennae or dishes installed near the home via satellite transmission to the locale.

Viasat Satellite home packages are 12Mbps, 50Mbps, and 100Mbps. The internet provider provides free installation between 3-5 business days. According to its promo advert, ViaSat satellite internet helps its clientele save more than $30 monthly.

6. GlobalTT

GlobalTT/ IPSEOS is a satellite internet provider in Kenya with over 30 years of experience in the country and beyond. The internet service provider offers reliable internet solutions to companies, NGOs, embassies, and other maritime organizations.

GlobalTT offers satellite internet and communication services through its Flyaway, Kymeta, Satcube, Thuraya, and Inmarsat antennas. At a fee of $70 for 60 GB and $150 for 150 GB monthly, the company offers internet speeds of up to 100Mbps up to 10 Gbps.

7. WafaNet

WafaNet is an internet provider that offers satellite broadband services everywhere across Kenya. The internet provider offers service plans from F5 at $59 plus $595 for dish installation to F100 at $1,180, with free installation. 

WafaNet satellite internet capabilities are shipped through its Hughes HX50/HX90 systems that incorporate TCP acceleration and advanced IP networking to offer customers the best reliable internet speed at an optimum fee, giving value for money.

8. OneNet Africa Limited

OneNet is an internet provider that offers satellite communications from hardware, IT solutions, and cyber security. With its headquarters stationed in Cyprus, OneNet has left a footprint in Africa by providing a one-stop list of satellite communications services for remote organizations.

Aside from satellite internet connectivity, OneNet is heavily invested in maritime, providing secure cloud-based solutions for ships and enterprise organizations at affordable fees.

9. Indigo Telecom

Indigo Telcom is amongst Africa's leading satellite-based telecommunication providers, providing some of the best internet solutions for governmental and corporate organizations. It has over 25 years of experience providing wireless solutions to consumers worldwide.

The company is now among the first generation of mobile technologies, leaving a mark in the fast-changing market with spiral demand for 5G technology services.

10. Safaricom

With spiralling hype around 5G internet connection, Kenya telecommunication giant Safaricom Plc moved to revolutionize and keep tabs with competitors by launching its satellite services.

In a partnership deal with Vodacom's mother company, Safaricom will offer satellite services with Texas-based company AST SpaceMobile. The satellite fees are reportedly estimated to retail at around Ksh 80,000 to keep tabs on competitors.

The move was made to tame competition already witnessed by the influx of multinational satellite internet providers. The move was necessitated by the need to maintain Safaricom Plc's share market, which has already been cemented through its voice and data services.
