Danielle Cormack reveals what she really thought of Wentworth's dramatic ending

Publish date: 2024-05-26

She starred in the award-winning drama Wentworth as Bea Smith between 2013-2016.

And on Wednesday, Danielle Cormack revealed what she really thought of the show's dramatic finale.

Speaking to Daily Mail Australia, the 50-year-old New Zealand Born actress said: 'I thought it was a monumental moment.'

Gorgeous: Wentworth star Danielle Cormack, 50, (pictured) has revealed what she really thought of the show's dramatic ending

'I watched the last episode, you know just say goodbye, I thought it was very moving.'

'To have all of those amazing actors that have been within the show right from the beginning, culminated in those final nine episodes, and then have that last episode, it was really moving.'

No stranger to attending award ceremonies, Danielle said she was thrilled to be back walking the red carpet of the 2021 AACTA awards.

'I'm feeling really relaxed, very comfortable and feeling most comfortable I've ever felt on the red carpet,' said Danielle. 

'It's so good to be here at the Opera House. I've never been to an awards at the Opera House.'

The award-winning actress, who is also known for playing iconic underworld figure Kate Leigh in the 2011 Underbelly series, packed a punch in a periwinkle blue power suit and metallic shoes for the evening.
