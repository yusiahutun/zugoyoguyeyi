19 Facts About Kandy Perahera

Publish date: 2024-09-15
Source: Beyonddreamtravels.com.au

The Kandy Perehera, also known as the Esala Perahera, is a grand procession that takes place annually in the city of Kandy, Sri Lanka. This vibrant and culturally significant event is considered one of the oldest and most spectacular religious festivals in the world. Spanning over ten nights, the Kandy Perehera attracts thousands of spectators from around the globe who come to witness the breathtaking display of tradition, spirituality, and pageantry. From the rhythmic beats of traditional drums to the flickering glow of ornate torches, this event is a feast for the senses. In this article, we will explore 19 fascinating facts about the Kandy Perehera, shedding light on its history, rituals, and significance in Sri Lankan culture.

The Kandy Perehera is an annual religious festival celebrated in Kandy, Sri Lanka.

Every year, thousands of devotees gather in Kandy to witness this grand spectacle of cultural and religious significance.

It is held in honor of the Sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha.

The festival serves as a tribute to the revered relic, which is believed to have been brought to Sri Lanka in the 4th century AD.

The origins of the Kandy Perehera can be traced back to the ancient kingdom of Kandy.

This festival has been celebrated for centuries and has deep historical roots in the region.

It is considered one of the grandest and oldest Buddhist festivals in Sri Lanka.

The Kandy Perehera attracts both local and international tourists who come to witness the vibrant processions and cultural performances.

The festival lasts for ten days and culminates in a majestic final procession known as the Randoli Perahera.

During this procession, the Sacred Tooth Relic is carried on the back of an ornately decorated elephant.

Over 100 elephants participate in the Randoli Perahera procession.

The sight of these majestic creatures adorned with colorful costumes and intricate jewelry is truly awe-inspiring.

The festival features a variety of traditional dances and music performances.

FAQs

1. What is Kandy Perehera?

Kandy Perehera is a religious and cultural festival held annually in the city of Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is a vibrant and colorful procession that showcases the sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha, believed to be enshrined in the Royal Palace complex.

2. When does Kandy Perehera take place?

Kandy Perehera is held over a period of ten days in the month of August. The exact dates vary each year based on the lunar calendar.

3. How long has Kandy Perehera been taking place?

Kandy Perehera has a history dating back over two centuries. It was first introduced by King Kirthi Sri Rajasinghe in the 18th century and has since become one of the most important cultural events in Sri Lanka.

4. What can visitors expect to see at Kandy Perehera?

Visitors can witness a grand procession with beautifully adorned elephants, traditional dances, fire performances, and various cultural rituals. The highlight of the event is the opportunity to see the sacred Tooth Relic being carried on the back of a majestic tusker elephant.

5. Can anyone participate in Kandy Perehera?

The procession is mainly carried out by the custodians of the temple and the traditional dancers and drummers. However, visitors can join the festivities, watch the processions, and immerse themselves in the festive atmosphere.

6. Is there an entrance fee to attend Kandy Perehera?

There is usually no entrance fee to witness the procession. However, donations are welcomed to support the upkeep of the temple and the continuation of this centuries-old tradition.

7. Are there any cultural practices or customs to be aware of while attending Kandy Perehera?

Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respectfully. It is also customary to remove footwear before entering religious sites and to behave in a manner that shows reverence for the sacred traditions being observed.

8. Can visitors take photographs during Kandy Perehera?

Photography is generally allowed during the festival. However, it is advised to be mindful of the cultural sensitivities and to avoid using flash photography, especially during the religious rituals.
