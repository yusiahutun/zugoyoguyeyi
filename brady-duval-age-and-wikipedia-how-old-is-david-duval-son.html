David Duval is an American professional golfer and former World No. 1 Golfer who played on the PGA Tour Champions. 

Born and raised in Florida, he graduated from Episcopal High School, where his early interest in golf was nurtured by his father, who served as a golf instructor.

Duval initiated his career by playing for the Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets men’s golf team, earning impressive accolades.

He won the U.S. Junior Amateur championship in 1989 and continued his success at Georgia Tech, becoming a four-time All-American.

He started his professional career in 1993 and secured his PGA Tour card in 1995.

Despite initial challenges, he triumphed with his first victory in 1997 and the 1999 and 2001 Championship.

Following his 2001 Open Championship victory, he faced declines in his career due to health and personal challenges. However, in 2022, he joined the PGA Tour Champions circuit after turning 50.

Alongside his playing career, Duval has been a TV golf commentator, contributing to ESPN and Golf Channel since 2012.
